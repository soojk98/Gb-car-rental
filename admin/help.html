User guide

Welcome. This guide explains how to run every part of the GB Car Rental system, step by step. Take your time — you can bookmark this page and come back any time.

1. Signing in and signing out

To sign in

  1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Safari, Edge).
  2. Type your website address, for example gbcarservices.com/login.html, and press Enter.
  3. You will see a page with two boxes: Email / Phone and Password.
  4. Type your email and password, then click the green Sign in button.
  5. You will be taken to the Dashboard automatically.

To sign out

  1. Look at the top-right corner of any page.
  2. You will see a circle with your initials (like "JS") and your name next to it.
  3. Click the Sign out button next to your name. You will return to the sign-in page.
Tip: Always sign out when you are using a shared computer. On your own computer it's fine to stay signed in.

2. The Dashboard — your daily summary

The Dashboard is the first page you see. It shows you everything that needs attention today.

The coloured boxes at the top

  • Active drivers — how many drivers are currently renting from you.
  • New leads — people who filled in the application form but you haven't contacted yet.
  • Cars in fleet / Available now — total cars, and how many are free for new rentals.
  • Active rentals — cars currently out with a driver.
  • Collected — [month] — total money received this month (green box).
  • Pending payments — payments drivers submitted that are waiting for your approval (yellow if any).
  • Pending deposits — drivers who uploaded a deposit slip waiting for your approval.
  • Pending driver docs — NRIC / License / PSV uploads waiting for you to check.
  • Low-stock items — oil filters or parts running low in your inventory (red if any).
Each box is clickable. Click it to jump to the full list.

The "Pending tasks" list

Below the boxes is a list of everything waiting for your action, broken into sections (driver docs, payments, deposits, expiring car documents, new leads, low stock). Each item has a blue Review button that takes you straight to the right place.

Start every day here. If every section says "✓ No items", you're up to date!

3. Managing Leads

A "lead" is a person who filled in the Apply to Drive form on your website but hasn't become a driver yet.

Viewing leads

  1. In the left sidebar, click Leads.
  2. You see all leads in a table. Each row has a status badge: new, contacted, converted, or rejected.
  3. Use the grey buttons at the top (All / New / Contacted / Converted / Rejected) to filter.
  4. Use the search box to find someone by name or phone.

Adding a lead manually

  1. Click the green + New lead button (top-right).
  2. Fill in Full name, WhatsApp, optional email, preferred car.
  3. Click Save.

After you call a lead

  1. Click Mark contacted on that row.
  2. Or click Edit to add notes (e.g. "Will come next Monday").

Turning a lead into a driver

  1. Click the blue Convert button on the lead's row.
  2. A window opens. Fill in:
    • Full name (already filled from the lead)
    • WhatsApp (already filled)
    • NRIC — this becomes the driver's login password.
    • Email — optional; if filled, Supabase emails them a one-click sign-in link.
  3. Click Convert.
  4. If you didn't enter an email, a box appears with a magic-link URL and the phone + NRIC password to share by WhatsApp.
Prefer no email? Leave it blank — then use the Sign-in link button on the Drivers page to share a one-tap WhatsApp link.

4. Managing Drivers

Viewing a driver's full profile

  1. Click Drivers in the sidebar.
  2. Click the blue View button on any row.
  3. You'll see everything about that driver on one page: profile, deposit, documents, agreement, rental history, and payments.

Common actions (buttons on each driver row)

  • Edit — change name, phone, status, deposit-required toggle, etc.
  • Documents — upload or view NRIC / License / PSV / bill uploads.
  • Agreement — upload the signed rental agreement PDF.
  • Review deposit (appears only when a deposit is submitted) — approve or reject with a reason.
  • Email invite — resend the magic-link email (only if the driver has an email).
  • Sign-in link — copy a one-time login URL or share via WhatsApp.
  • Delete — remove the driver (be careful — this is permanent).

Approving driver documents

  1. Driver uploads e.g. NRIC in the driver portal → status is pending.
  2. Dashboard's "Pending driver docs" box shows 1.
  3. Click Review, which opens Drivers. Click Documents on that driver.
  4. In the list, pending docs show Approve and Reject buttons.
  5. Click Approve if the document looks good; or Reject and type a reason.
If the driver re-uploads a new file, the old approved one stays until you approve the new one. Rejected versions are kept as history.

Approving a deposit

  1. Driver uploads bank-in slip → status becomes submitted.
  2. Click Review deposit.
  3. View the slip, check amount matches, click Approve (or Reject with a reason).

5. Managing Cars

Adding a new car to your fleet

  1. Click Cars in the sidebar.
  2. Click + Add car.
  3. Fill in plate number, model, year, colour, weekly rate, mileage, and condition (new or used).
  4. Fill in purchase price, purchase date, and registration date — these are used later by the Profit & Loss page to calculate your return on investment.
  5. Upload front, back, side photos (optional but useful).
  6. Click Save.

Uploading compliance documents (registration, road tax, insurance, Puspakom)

  1. Click the Documents button on a car's row.
  2. Choose document type.
  3. Choose the file (photo or PDF).
  4. Enter the expiry date (required for road tax, insurance, Puspakom).
  5. Click Upload.
Why expiry dates matter: the system warns you 30 days before a document expires on the Dashboard. Without an expiry date, you get no warning.

Viewing everything about a car (including logs and financials)

  1. Click the blue View button on a car's row.
  2. You see overview, photos, compliance docs, financials summary, and full rental history.
  3. Click Open logs at the top to record mileage / maintenance / summons / accidents.

6. Rentals and collecting payments

Creating a new rental

  1. Click Rentals in the sidebar.
  2. Click + New rental.
  3. Pick a driver. The deposit amount auto-fills based on the driver's approved deposit (or zero if waived).
  4. Pick an available car. The weekly rate auto-fills from the car.
  5. Pick a start date.
  6. Click Create rental. The car is marked as Rented.

Recording a payment yourself

  1. Find the rental in the list (default filter is active).
  2. Click the Payments button.
  3. In the modal, enter amount, paid-on date, method (cash / transfer / cheque).
  4. Period Start and End auto-fill to the Mon–Sun week around the paid date — you can change them.
  5. Click + Add payment. The total collected updates immediately.

Approving a payment the driver submitted

  1. Dashboard's "Pending payments" box shows a count > 0.
  2. Click that box (or go to Rentals → Payments on the relevant rental).
  3. Pending rows have Approve / Reject buttons and a Slip link.
  4. Click Slip to view the bank-in proof, then Approve or Reject with a reason.

Ending a rental

  1. Click End on the rental row.
  2. Enter the end date.
  3. Tick Deposit returned to driver if you've refunded it.
  4. Click End rental. The car is marked Available again.

7. Sending a payment link to a driver

Instead of receiving a WhatsApp message with a bank slip, you can send drivers a special link that lets them upload the slip themselves, without logging in.

  1. Click Payment Link in the sidebar.
  2. Optional: pick the driver's car plate from the dropdown to pre-fill the plate for them.
  3. Click Share via WhatsApp — this opens WhatsApp with a ready message.
  4. The driver opens the link, types their plate (if not pre-filled), enters the amount, paid date, method, and uploads the slip.
  5. The payment appears as pending for you to approve in Rentals → Payments.

8. Inventory (oil filters, parts)

Adding an item

  1. Click Inventory → + Item.
  2. Enter name, SKU (optional), category (e.g. "oil filter"), unit (pcs, L, etc.), reorder level.
  3. Click Save.

Recording a purchase (stock-in)

  1. Click + Movement.
  2. Type: Stock in (purchase).
  3. Pick item, quantity, unit cost. Total auto-fills.
  4. Optionally upload the receipt.
  5. Click Save.

Recording usage (stock-out)

  1. Click + Movement → Type: Stock out.
  2. Pick the item, quantity used, and (optional) the car it was used for.
  3. Save. Stock count goes down automatically.
If stock goes at or below the reorder level, the item row turns red and the Dashboard shows a low-stock warning.

9. Profit & Loss — are you making money?

Click Profit & Loss in the sidebar. For each car you see:

  • Revenue — total money collected from rentals of this car.
  • Costs — maintenance + unpaid summons + accident repairs.
  • Net P&L — revenue minus costs minus purchase price.
  • Recouped % — how much of the purchase price you've earned back.
  • Years to recoup — estimate of how many more years at your current earning rate.
  • Years left (15-yr limit) — Malaysia's e-hailing cars must retire at 15 years from registration.
  • Projected lifetime profit — net today plus expected income until year-15.
For accurate numbers, make sure each car has purchase price, purchase date, and registration date filled in (Cars → Edit).

10. Yearly financial documents

  1. Click Financial in the sidebar.
  2. Click + Upload document.
  3. Pick the year, choose type (Financial Statement, Tax Computation, or Tax Return Form).
  4. Attach the PDF and save.

The page groups files by year. Each year has three slots. You can come back any time and click View to open a file.

11. Adding more admin users

If you want someone else (a manager, a relative) to help manage the system:

  1. Click Users in the sidebar.
  2. Click + New admin.
  3. Enter name, email, and a strong password (at least 8 characters).
  4. Click Create.
  5. Send the email + password to that person. They sign in at /login.html.

Other actions you can do per user:

  • Reset password — lets you set a new password (for example, if they forgot it).
  • Deactivate — disables the account so they cannot sign in (useful if they leave).
  • Activate — re-enables a deactivated account.
  • Delete — permanently remove the account.
You cannot deactivate or delete your own account. Ask another admin to do it if you really need to.

12. What drivers see in their portal

When a driver signs in at /login.html with their phone + NRIC (or clicks their magic link), they land on their own simpler portal:

  • My Rental — their current car, weekly rate, total paid, outstanding balance.
  • Documents — upload NRIC / License / PSV / bills. You approve them.
  • Deposit — upload bank-in slip for the deposit.
  • Agreement — view signed agreement you uploaded.
  • Payments — submit weekly rental payments (with proof of payment). You approve.
  • Logs — reference info.

13. Common problems and fixes

A driver says "I can't log in"

  • Check the Users page — is their account marked Inactive? If yes, click Activate.
  • Click Reset password and send them the new password by WhatsApp.
  • Or use the Sign-in link on the Drivers page to generate a fresh one-tap magic link.

A driver uploaded a payment but I don't see it

  • Go to Dashboard → Pending payments box → click it.
  • Or: Rentals → find the rental → Payments → scroll down, pending rows will be at the top.

Document shows "Not on record" on driver portal even though I uploaded it

  • Make sure the document expiry date is filled in on Cars → Documents.
  • If no expiry, the driver portal shows "Uploaded · [date]" — also a good sign.

Deposit or payment approve button does nothing

  • Reload the page (press Ctrl + Shift + R on Windows, Cmd + Shift + R on Mac). This forces the browser to load the latest version of the site.

Low-stock warning but I just purchased

  • Go to Inventory → + Movement → Stock in → pick the item and enter the quantity. The stock is only updated when you record a movement.

I forgot my own password

  • Ask another admin user to go to Users → your row → Reset password.
  • If you are the only admin, contact whoever set up your Supabase project (technical support) to reset it from the Supabase dashboard.

If something is not working and this guide doesn't answer it, call your technical support and show them which page you were on and what you clicked.
